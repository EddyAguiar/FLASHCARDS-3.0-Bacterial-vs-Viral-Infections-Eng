What is the fundamental difference between bacteria and viruses?
Bacteria: Fully independent unicellular organisms that can reproduce on their own.
Viruses: Not cells; require a host cell to replicate.
These differences determine treatment: antibiotics act on bacteria, not viruses.

How does a typical bacterial infection present?
Sudden onset, localized symptoms (e.g., ear pain), high and persistent fever, thick yellow/green discharge.
Examples: bacterial otitis, strep throat, bacterial sinusitis.

How does a viral infection typically present?
Gradual onset, systemic symptoms (runny nose, cough, body aches), moderate fever, clear nasal discharge.
Examples: common cold, flu, viral pharyngitis.
